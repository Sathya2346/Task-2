Orange

An orange is a citrus fruit with a leathery skin and juicy flesh. It is a good source of vitamin C.

Parts of an orange

Peel: The waxy skin of an orange that protects the juice sacs inside. The peel has two layers: the flavedo, which is thin and orange, and the albedo, which is thick and fibrous.

Endocarp: The edible part of the orange, which includes the segments, core, and membrane. The segments contain juice sacs, which are held together by a waxy substance.

Juice: Contains sugars, acids, vitamins, minerals, and other components.
